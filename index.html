What is this?

This is a simple orbit calculator, minus the gravity part.

It calculates using Delta Time where on the circular orbit the planet should be.

To do this, we parse our delta time into our parametric formula with t being time.

Main Problems I Faced

üî¢ Cartesian Formula üìê

Initially I used a cartesian formula to calcualate the points.

When we reached our boundaries, I would switch the formula from a plus to a negative.

Unfortunately, although the displacement was correct, the velocity would not be consistent.

The closer the planet got to the x boundary, the faster it would go.

When it went past the x boundary, then the Y coordinate was justifiably null or abnormal, resulting in glitches.

Parametric Equations Cartesian Equations

As you can see, these make the orbits be a consistent speed, with no glitches at the boundaries.

( acos(t) , asin(t) ) = ( x , y )

where a is radius.

This causes glitches when x reaches it's limits, as going past that point for x causes y to be invalid / Integer.Max.

(x - a)2 + (y ‚àí b)2 = r2

where r is radius.

üí´ Draw Orbit üí´

I use the Draw Debug Circle built into UE4.

This allowed me to focus on movement of the planets, rather than drawing the orbit.

The two problems I faced was drawing an elliptical orbit, and drawing an orbit at an angle.


The latter was solved by attaching this component to a different parent, the parent of the orbit of that planet.

As it was already at an angle due to how we calculate that planet's orbit, it drew at an angle too.

(Side Note: For this planet, we update the relative position and not the absolute position.

This allows us to keep our inherited rotation.)

Drawing an elliptical orbit was easier, we modified out YAxis and ZAxis scales to fit our orbit.
